Mama Hamisi Menu

Appetizers

  • Mahamri

    Sweet, triangular-shaped fried bread made with coconut milk.

  • Bhajia

    Slices of potatoes dipped in gram flour (besan) batter mixed with spices, then deep-fried until golden and crispy.

  • Samosas (Sambusa)

    Triangular-shaped pastries filled with spiced meat (often beef or chicken) or vegetables.

Main Courses

  • Pilau

    Pilau is a staple in Swahili cuisine, especially served during celebrations and special occasions.

  • Biryani

    Another spiced rice dish, but biryani differs from pilau in its layering of flavors. It often involves marinated meat (chicken, beef, or lamb) cooked with rice and served with accompaniments like chutney or raita (yogurt sauce).

  • Samaki wa Kupaka

    A popular coastal dish where fish (often whole) is grilled or fried and then cooked in a rich coconut sauce flavored with spices like turmeric, garlic, and ginger. Itâ€™s typically served with rice or chapati.

Desserts

  • Kaimati

    Sweet, fried dough balls coated in sugar syrup or honey. These are crunchy on the outside and soft on the inside, making them a popular treat during special occasions or holidays.

  • Vitumbua

    Rice flour doughnuts that are soft and spongy, often flavored with coconut and cardamom.

  • Halwa

    A rich, jelly-like dessert made from sugar, ghee, and flavored with cardamom and saffron.
